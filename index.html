This website gives me an opportunity to record some of my experiences while learning more about computer science and software engineering.

Education

As a scientist, I saw more and more of the career opportunities in science move away from the traditional discplines and towards software. This was not necessarily a bad thing for me as I had been interested in computers since I was in high school. In fact, when taking high school math, I was more intesete in programming the calculators than learning the math. Fortunately for me, I was also able to excel in math while taking it in college. Eventually, I returned to college for computer science, earning a Master of Science in Computer Science.

Work Experience

I started my career working in one of the more traditional disciplines in science. After several years of experience in this scientific discipline, I found a job coding information from this discipline's associated literature. Hence the name Scientist of Information. After gaining knowledge about my subject area as an information scientist, I took on the role of data steward for the a forementioned subject area. As a data steward, I have the opportunity to work with artifacts such as the Domain Model and the Data Dictionary.

Hobbies

  • Arduino: While in school for computer science, I became interested in programming arduino microprocessors. I enjoyed the process of coding something and watching the results in the physical world.
  • HTML and CSS: After graduation, I decided to learn more about HTML and CSS as a way to implement and expand upon some of the skills that I had learned in college.
